The model learned by reading many small notes.
It learned that words often arrive in patterns.
It learned that a sentence can begin with an idea and end with a surprise.

A language model predicts the next token from the tokens that came before it.
Small models remember short habits.
Large models remember richer shapes.
Every model is a mirror made of statistics.

When the prompt says hello, the model looks for the paths that often follow hello.
When the prompt asks a question, the model tries to continue in a helpful voice.
The better the training text, the better the continuation.

Build small things first.
Make them run.
Make them understandable.
Then make them larger.
